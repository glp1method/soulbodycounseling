I became a therapist because I believe that healing is not a linear path — and that the people most often failed by systems deserve the most thoughtful, affirming care. My work is rooted in the conviction that you already hold the wisdom you need; therapy is the space where we excavate it.

I've spent my career working with LGBTQ+ communities, trauma survivors, and individuals navigating substance use — not as separate "populations," but as whole human beings whose lives don't fit neatly into a single category. I don't pathologize; I accompany.

My training spans Gestalt therapy, EMDR, Motivational Interviewing, and guided imagery. But what I bring to the room is more than technique — it's presence, curiosity, and an unwillingness to pretend that healing happens without acknowledging the systems and structures that shape your life.

I see clients in-person in Denver and virtually across Colorado. I accept both insurance and private pay.

What I Believe

The values that guide this work

01

You are the expert on your own life

My role is not to tell you who you are or should be. It's to help you reconnect with the knowing you already carry.

02

Affirming care isn't optional — it's essential

LGBTQ+ identities are not problems to be solved. Queer joy, queer grief, queer complexity all belong in the therapy room.

03

The body holds the story

Integrative therapy means we don't leave your nervous system out of the conversation. Healing happens in the whole person.

04

Shame has no seat at this table

Whether you're navigating addiction, trauma, or something you've never said out loud — this is a judgment-free space.
